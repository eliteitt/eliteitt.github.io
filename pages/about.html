About Us

Nothing could be further from the truth than the assumption that all interpreters (who work with spoken language) / translators (who work with written language) are the same. There is a significant difference in quality between a so-so interpreter's / translator's and a good one's performance.

At Elite ITT, we pride ourselves on our technically and artistically advanced interpreting, translating as well as teaching. With our combination of academic qualifications, practical experience and expertise, we will most effectively help you overcome any barriers to cross-linguistic and cross-cultural communication.

Dr Giao (Grace) Quynh Tran

Founder
Principal Interpreter, Translator & Teacher

Dr Tran has a PhD in Applied Linguistics from the University of Melbourne, an MA in English (Applied Linguistics) and a Graduate Certificate in Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages (TESOL) from University of Wisconsin, Madison, WI, USA, a Graduate Diploma in TESOL from Canberra University and a BA in English from Vietnam National University. She is also a NAATI Professional Interpreter and Translator in English and Vietnamese, both directions.

As an interpreter / translator, Dr Tran has a lot of experience in legal, medical and community interpreting / translating as well as in simultaneous / conference interpreting. She has been known as the most high-profile Vietnamese interpreter. With her strong background in linguistics and what her colleagues have referred to as her interpreting / translating talent, she has made a very good impression on legal and medical practitioners, politicians (e.g. a Prime Minister, Ministers, Members of Parliament, community leaders, etc.), delegates, businessmen, VIPs, TV audiences, etc. with her consecutive / simultaneous interpreting skills at the courts, in high-level conferences / workshops as well as on live TV. In her interpreting / translating career, not a single client is less than happy with her work.

As a teacher, Dr Tran has four years of experience teaching English (TESOL) as a staff member in the Department of English Linguistics and Literature, School of Social Sciences and Humanities at Vietnam National University, and several periods of teaching English as a Second Language (ESL) / tutoring in Linguistics and Applied Linguistics in Vietnam, the U.S. and Australia. She has been teaching postgraduate interpreting and translation as well as supervising MA theses in TESOL at the School of Humanities and Communication Arts, Western Sydney University. Her creative teaching and effective supervision have been highly complimented upon by her students and her colleagues.

As a researcher, Dr Tran has a number of publications and presentations (See details under Research). She has presented a workshop about Court Interpreting at AUSIT, where she was praised for her expertise. Her article on court interpreting in the Australian Law Society Journal (LSJ, April 2018, Issue 43) has also attracted very positive feedback. She was also invited to present about Court Interpreting on Bench TV.
